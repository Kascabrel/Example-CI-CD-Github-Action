About Me

Resident Neurosurgeon | Humanitarian | Advocate for Pediatric Neurosurgery in Africa

Professional Journey

I am currently a fifth-year resident in Neurosurgery at the University of Nairobi, training at Kenyatta National Hospital. My focus is on pediatric neurosurgery where I manage cases such as hydrocephalus, spina bifida, brain tumors, and neurotrauma.

Previously, I served as a Medical Officer at Mogodé District Hospital in Northern Cameroon. Despite harsh conditions and lack of equipment, I established the first operating theatre and performed life-saving surgeries. I am deeply committed to bringing accessible pediatric neurosurgical care to underserved populations.

Qualifications & Experience

  • 5th year Neurosurgery Resident, University of Nairobi
  • Medical Officer, Mogodé District Hospital (2017–2020)
  • Internships in Internal Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, and OB/GYN
  • Coordinator of community health activities in Ekona Mbengue

Conferences & Courses

  • SNOSSA 2023 Conference - Nairobi
  • EAANS 2023 - Mombasa
  • CAANS Congress 2022 - Nairobi
  • Workshop Chair - Spinal Dysraphism, Far North Cameroon (2024)
  • Ethics of Research Training - Buea, Cameroon

Advocacy & Vision

My ongoing research aims to evaluate the knowledge and practices related to pediatric neural tube defects in the Far North of Cameroon. I believe education, early diagnosis, and advocacy can save thousands of children each year. I envision a future where every child in Africa has access to quality neurosurgical care.
