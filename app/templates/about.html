About Me

Resident Neurosurgeon | Humanitarian | Advocate for Pediatric Neurosurgery in Africa

Professional Journey

I'm a fifth-year Neurosurgery resident at the University of Nairobi, currently training at Kenyatta National Hospital with a focus on pediatric neurosurgery. I manage cases like hydrocephalus, spina bifida, neurotrauma, and brain tumors.

Formerly at Mogodé District Hospital in Northern Cameroon, I led life-saving surgical interventions in a resource-limited setting and established its first operating theatre. My mission is to expand access to pediatric neurosurgical care across underserved communities in Africa.

🎓 Qualifications & Experience

  • 5th-year Neurosurgery Resident, University of Nairobi
  • Medical Officer, Mogodé District Hospital (2017–2020)
  • Internships in Internal Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, and OB/GYN
  • Community Health Coordinator in Ekona Mbengue

📚 Conferences & Courses

  • SNOSSA 2023 – Nairobi
  • EAANS 2023 – Mombasa
  • CAANS Congress 2022 – Nairobi
  • Workshop Chair – Spinal Dysraphism, Cameroon (2024)
  • Ethics of Research Training – Buea, Cameroon

🌍 Advocacy & Vision

I’m researching neural tube defects among children in Northern Cameroon. My goal is to raise awareness, improve early diagnosis, and ensure every African child can access quality neurosurgical care. Education and advocacy are powerful tools to save lives.
